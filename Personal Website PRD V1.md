# **taotang.io — Personal Website**

## **Product Definition & User Use Case Documentation**

This document defines **what the personal website is**, **what it is not**, and **how it is used** by different categories of users.

It is written as a **product document**, not marketing copy.  
Its purpose is to prevent scope drift, over-engineering, and misalignment between intent and execution.

This document should remain valid even if visual design, copy, or tooling changes.  
---

## **1\. Product Overview**

### **Product Name**

**taotang.io** (Personal Website)

### **Product Type**

**Personal Conversion System**

### **Primary Function**

To convert inbound interest into **qualified, paid, scoped work** with minimal manual sales overhead.

### **Secondary Functions**

* Establish credibility and trust with non-technical buyers

* Provide a safe, structured entry point for urgent or advisory needs

* Filter out misaligned opportunities early

* Serve as a durable professional anchor across career transitions

---

## **2\. Core Product Philosophy**

### **Guiding Principle**

The website exists to make it easy for the *right* people to engage — and easy for the *wrong* people to disengage.

### **Non-Goals (Explicit)**

The website is **not** intended to:

* Maximize traffic

* Rank on SEO

* Act as a personal blog

* Pitch vague AI strategy

* Function as a SaaS product

* Replace human judgment in deal qualification

Any feature that does not support conversion, qualification, or clarity is out of scope.

---

## **3\. Target User Groups (Product View)**

The website supports **multiple user groups**, each with distinct motivations.

### **User Group A — Non-Technical Decision Makers**

**Examples:**

* HR / Talent leaders

* Program managers

* Operations leaders

* Compliance or risk stakeholders

* Innovation or transformation offices

**Primary Needs:**

* Confidence in expertise

* Risk reduction

* External validation / stamp of approval

* Clear engagement structure

* Predictable cost and scope

**Key Pain Points:**

* Cannot evaluate technical depth directly

* Fear making the wrong hire

* Need defensible decisions internally

* Time pressure and ambiguity

The website must:

* Signal credibility quickly

* Reduce perceived risk

* Make engagement feel safe and bounded

---

### **User Group B — Semi-Technical / Technical Sponsors**

**Examples:**

* Product managers

* Engineering managers

* Data science leads

* Platform owners

**Primary Needs:**

* Help unblocking delivery

* Independent technical perspective

* Fast execution without politics

* Someone who can own outcomes

**Key Pain Points:**

* Internal bandwidth constraints

* Unclear architecture or direction

* Prior failed attempts

The website must:

* Emphasize execution and outcomes

* Avoid buzzwords

* Demonstrate real system-building experience

---

### **User Group C — Urgent / Advisory Seekers**

**Examples:**

* Recruiters or HR partners with urgent client needs

* Internal teams needing a quick second opinion

* Stakeholders under deadline pressure

**Primary Needs:**

* Speed

* Authority

* Clarity

**Key Pain Points:**

* Lack of time

* High uncertainty

The website must:

* Offer a fast, paid entry point

* Avoid friction

* Clearly define boundaries

---

## **4\. Core User Journeys (Use Cases)**

### **Use Case 1 — HR / Program-Led Project Engagement**

**Scenario:**

A non-technical leader is tasked with finding external AI expertise for a project but lacks internal evaluators.

**User Journey:**

1. User lands on taotang.io

2. Immediately understands:

   * Who Tao is

   * What kind of work he does

   * That engagements are structured and safe

3. Reviews examples of project-based offerings

4. Gains confidence through clear process explanation

5. Completes intake form

6. Receives structured follow-up

**Outcome:**

* Engagement moves forward without prolonged back-and-forth

* Buyer feels protected and justified

---

### **Use Case 2 — Technical Sponsor Seeking Execution Help**

**Scenario:**

A PM or EM knows *something is wrong* with their AI system but lacks time or clarity to fix it.

**User Journey:**

1. User scans offerings

2. Recognizes a problem-solution match

3. Understands scope, timeline, and deliverables

4. Submits intake with technical context

5. Moves directly to scoped engagement

**Outcome:**

* Reduced friction

* Faster decision-making

* Clear ownership transfer

---

### **Use Case 3 — Urgent Advisory / Short Consultation**

**Scenario:**

A recruiter or stakeholder needs senior input within hours or days.

**User Journey:**

1. User identifies advisory option

2. Understands it is paid, time-boxed, and limited

3. Books or submits request

4. Receives immediate confirmation

**Outcome:**

* Fast revenue

* Controlled scope

* Potential funnel into larger work

---

## **5\. Product Features (Functional Definition)**

### **5.1 Homepage**

**Purpose:**

* Immediate clarity

* Credibility signaling

* Directional routing

**Functional Requirements:**

* Clear statement of what problems are solved

* Clear call to action

* No long biography

---

### **5.2 Offer Presentation**

**Purpose:**

* Make work purchasable

* Reduce ambiguity

**Functional Requirements:**

* Fixed engagement shapes

* Explicit deliverables

* Explicit exclusions

---

### **5.3 Proof of Execution**

**Purpose:**

* Counter skepticism

* Demonstrate real-world execution

**Functional Requirements:**

* Real shipped example(s)

* Focus on decisions and tradeoffs

* Avoid NDA-sensitive details

---

### **5.4 Engagement Process Explanation**

**Purpose:**

* Reduce anxiety

* Establish expectations

**Functional Requirements:**

* Explain how work is done

* Clarify communication model

* State access requirements

---

### **5.5 Smart Intake System**

**Purpose:**

* Qualify leads

* Route intent

* Reduce manual coordination

**Functional Requirements:**

* Capture core context

* Identify engagement type

* Integrate with payment where applicable

**Design Constraints:**

* Lightweight

* Transparent

* No opaque automation

---

## **6\. Product Boundaries & Guardrails**

### **What the Website Must Enforce**

* Fixed scope thinking

* Respect for access constraints

* Paid engagement norms

### **What the Website Must Avoid**

* Open-ended consulting promises

* Over-automation

* Excessive feature creep

---

## **7\. Success Metrics (Product-Level)**

The website is successful if:

* It attracts fewer but higher-quality inquiries

* Users reference specific offerings

* Incompatible leads self-filter

* Conversion does not require heavy persuasion

Traffic volume is irrelevant.

---

## **8\. Evolution (Explicitly Deferred)**

Future additions may include:

* Deeper case studies

* Technical write-ups

* Automation enhancements

These are **out of scope** until consistent paid engagement occurs.

---

## **9\. Final Product Definition**

taotang.io is a **personal, productized interface** between Tao’s expertise and real buyer needs.

It is designed to be calm, credible, and decisive — not impressive, loud, or speculative.

If the site feels narrow, that is intentional.
